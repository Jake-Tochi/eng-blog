I Used to Be Afraid of Speaking with Foreigners

My first cross-cultural exchange

The anxiety before my first international exchange

Trying anything for the first time makes you nervous, right? Today I'd like to share the experience of the first time I had a meal with a foreigner. It was about 15 years ago. A college friend brought her Australian boyfriend, and we decided to have dinner together. Back then I could hardly speak English, and I was so anxious I barely slept for two nights leading up to it.

The nerve-wracking dinner

On the day, the four of us—my friend, her boyfriend, my wife and I—went out for dinner. The other three could speak English, so the conversation flowed from the start. As for me, I didn't know what to do and stayed tense the whole time (I must have excused myself to the restroom more times than I can count…).

A moment of kindness

But he was incredibly kind. He slowed his speech for me and made sure everyone could enjoy the conversation. Thanks to that, by the end I felt, "Maybe speaking with foreigners isn't so scary after all." That experience taught me that even when you feel anxious about unfamiliar cultures or languages, your heart relaxes naturally when the other person tries to meet you halfway.

"Maybe speaking with foreigners isn't so scary after all."

What's normal in a diverse society

I later realized that yes, he was a kind person, but speaking slowly and being considerate is simply normal in diverse societies. When you live alongside people from many backgrounds, you often need to communicate with those who aren't fluent, so adjusting your pace and style is essential.
